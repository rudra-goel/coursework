Visible Spectroscopy concepts and tools

Visible spectroscopy is used to observe electronic transitions in atoms and a few specialized molecules.

Atomic Emission Spectra

Excited atoms emit light at discrete wavelengths. To see this in action, try putting a solution of a metal salt in a flame! Electrons are excited to higher energy levels by the flame, and they emit light as they return to the ground state. Many of these transitions have energies that correspond to the visible region of the electromagnetic spectrum. The differences in energy between levels depend on the identity of the atom, so an atomic spectrum can serve as a "fingerprint" for the elements.
